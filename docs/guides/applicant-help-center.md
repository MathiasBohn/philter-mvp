## Creating Your Account

### Step 1: Receive Your Invitation
Your broker or the building will send you an email invitation to create your Philter account. The email will come from `invitations@philter.com`.

**Subject:** "You're invited to apply to [Building Name]"

**What to do:**
1. Click the "Create Account" link in the email
2. The link expires in 7 days - create your account promptly
3. Check spam/junk folder if you don't see the email within 30 minutes

---

### Step 2: Set Up Your Account
1. Click the invitation link
2. Enter your email address (should be pre-filled)
3. Create a strong password:
   - At least 8 characters
   - Include uppercase, lowercase, number, and symbol
   - Don't use common passwords
4. Click "Create Account"

---

### Step 3: Verify Your Email
1. Check your email for verification message
2. Click "Verify Email Address"
3. You'll be redirected to Philter login page
4. Log in with your email and password

**Tip:** Add philter.com to your contacts so our emails don't go to spam

## Understanding the Application Process

### Overview
The Philter application has 11 sections:

1. **Application Info** - Basic details about the property
2. **Personal Information** - Your contact and identification info
3. **Employment** - Your job history and income
4. **Financial Information** - Your assets and liabilities
5. **Real Estate Holdings** - Properties you own (if any)
6. **Documents** - Upload required files
7. **References** - Landlord and personal references
8. **Acknowledgements** - Authorization forms (8 forms)
9. **Building Policies** - Review and acknowledge building rules
10. **Personal Introduction** - Your cover letter to the board
11. **Review & Submit** - Final review before submission

### Common Questions

**Q: What if I just started a new job?**
A: That's fine! Enter your new job with the start date. If you're within first 3 months, also include your previous job to show employment history.

**Q: What if I'm unemployed?**
A: You can still apply, but you'll need to explain your income source (savings, family support, etc.) in your cover letter and show strong financial reserves.

**Q: What if I have multiple jobs (side gigs)?**
A: Add each job separately. Total income from all jobs will be calculated automatically.

**Q: Why do you need my supervisor's contact info?**
A: Buildings typically verify employment by calling your supervisor or HR department. Having contact info speeds up the verification process.

**Q: What if my employer doesn't allow employment verification calls?**
A: No problem - you can upload an employment verification letter from HR instead in the Documents section.

### Financial Summary

After entering assets and liabilities, you'll see your **Financial Summary**:

**Calculated Automatically:**
- **Total Assets:** Sum of all your assets
- **Total Liabilities:** Sum of all your debts
- **Net Worth:** Assets - Liabilities
- **Liquid Assets:** Cash + Savings + Stocks (easily accessible)
- **Debt-to-Income Ratio (DTI):** Monthly debt payments / Monthly income
- **Liquid Assets to Rent Ratio:** How many months/years rent you could pay from liquid assets

**Color Coding:**
- üü¢ **Green:** Excellent financials
- üü° **Yellow:** Acceptable financials
- üî¥ **Red:** Potential concern (but not automatic disqualification)

## Acknowledgements

### What Are Acknowledgements?

These are authorization forms that give permission for various checks and verifications. Think of them as legal consent forms.

**There are 8 acknowledgement forms** (or 7 if building doesn't allow pets):

1. Personal Information Authorization
2. Credit Check Authorization
3. Background Check Consent
4. Reference Contact Authorization
5. Employment Verification Authorization
6. Financial Statement Verification
7. Pet Ownership Declaration (if building allows pets)
8. Move-in Date Commitment

**All are required** - you cannot submit your application without completing all acknowledgements.

---

### How to Complete Acknowledgements

**For Each Acknowledgement:**

**Step 1: Read Carefully**
- Read the full text of the acknowledgement
- Understand what you're authorizing
- Ask questions if anything is unclear

**Step 2: Check the Box**
- ‚òë Check "I acknowledge and agree"
- The checkbox won't be available until you've scrolled through the text

**Step 3: Electronic Signature**
- Type your full legal name in the signature field
- Today's date will auto-fill
- This is legally binding (same as handwritten signature)

**Step 4: Save**
- Click "Save" or it saves automatically
- Timestamp is recorded

**Repeat for all 8 acknowledgements!**

---

### Understanding Each Acknowledgement

#### 1. Personal Information Authorization
**What it means:** You authorize the building to collect, use, and verify your personal information for evaluating your application.

**What you're agreeing to:**
- Building can verify the information you provided
- Information may be shared with board, managing agent, verification services
- All information must be true and accurate

---

#### 2. Credit Check Authorization
**What it means:** You authorize the building to pull your credit report.

**What you're agreeing to:**
- Hard inquiry on your credit report (may temporarily lower score by a few points)
- Building will see your credit history, score, payment history
- Information kept confidential

**Note:** You'll need to enter your Social Security Number for credit check

---

#### 3. Background Check Consent
**What it means:** You authorize a background check including criminal and civil records.

**What you're agreeing to:**
- Criminal history search
- Civil court records search
- Eviction history search
- Sex offender registry check
- Terrorist watch list screening

**Your rights:**
- If denied due to background check, you'll be notified
- You can dispute inaccurate information
- Background information kept confidential

---

#### 4. Reference Contact Authorization
**What it means:** You authorize the building to contact your references.

**What you're agreeing to:**
- Building can call/email your landlord references
- Building can call/email your personal references
- References can disclose information about you
- You release references from liability

---

#### 5. Employment Verification Authorization
**What it means:** You authorize the building to verify your employment.

**What you're agreeing to:**
- Building can contact your current/former employers
- Employers can disclose employment dates, position, income
- Standard part of application process

---

#### 6. Financial Statement Verification
**What it means:** You authorize the building to verify your financial information.

**What you're agreeing to:**
- Building can contact your banks/financial institutions
- Institutions can disclose account balances and history
- You may need to provide additional documentation

**Note:** Most buildings verify through documents you upload rather than contacting banks directly

---

#### 7. Pet Ownership Declaration
**When required:** Only if building allows pets

**What it covers:**
- Do you own pets? (Yes/No)
- If yes, details about each pet:
  - Type (dog, cat, bird, etc.)
  - Breed
  - Weight
  - Age
  - Name
  - Service/emotional support animal (Yes/No, documentation required)
- Acknowledgement of pet policy
- Agreement to pay pet deposit and monthly fees

**If you have pets:**
- Be honest! Undisclosed pets can result in lease violation or eviction
- Ensure your pets comply with building policy (type, breed, size, weight restrictions)
- If you have service/emotional support animal, upload documentation

**If building doesn't allow pets:**
- Check "I do not own pets"
- Do NOT get a pet after approval without board permission

---

#### 8. Move-in Date Commitment
**What it covers:**
- Your proposed move-in date (must be 30+ days out)
- Acknowledgement of move-in requirements:
  - Move-in permitted only on specific days/times (e.g., weekdays 9am-5pm)
  - Must reserve service elevator 48 hours in advance
  - May require move-in deposit (refundable)
  - Must provide proof of renter's/homeowner's insurance before move-in
- Commitment to be available for proposed date or mutually agreed alternative

**Choosing Your Move-In Date:**
- Be realistic (applications take 2-8 weeks to approve)
- Propose date at least 30 days out
- Check building's move-in rules (some buildings restrict to weekdays only)

### Common Questions About Acknowledgements

**Q: Do I really need to complete all of them?**
A: Yes - all acknowledgements are required. Application cannot be submitted without them.

**Q: Is the electronic signature legal?**
A: Yes - electronic signatures are legally binding under federal law (ESIGN Act).

**Q: What if I don't want to authorize credit check?**
A: Credit check is standard requirement for all applications. Without it, application will be denied.

**Q: Can I skip background check?**
A: No - background check is required. If you have concerns about your background, address them proactively in your cover letter.

**Q: What if I don't have any pets but might get one later?**
A: Check "no pets" for now. If you want to get a pet after approval, you'll need to request board permission.

**Q: What if my proposed move-in date changes?**
A: You can update it later. This is just your initial target date.

## Building Policies

### What Are Building Policies?

Before you start your application, you'll see a screen showing the building's policies and rules. This ensures you understand all requirements before investing time in the application.

**Policy Sections:**
1. **Pet Policy** - Pets allowed? Types, breeds, sizes, fees
2. **Alteration Policy** - Rules for renovations/changes to apartment
3. **Subletting Policy** - Can you sublet? For how long? Approval process?

- RFIs are common and don't mean denial
- Simply means admin needs clarification or additional documents

**How to Respond:**

**Step 1: Read Carefully**
- Log in to Philter
- Read RFI request carefully
- Note deadline (typically 3 business days)

**Step 2: Gather What's Needed**
- Collect requested documents

- Ensure documents are legible

**Step 4: Confirm**
- You'll receive confirmation email

- Process continues

**Common RFI Requests:**

- Fast response = faster approval

### Financial

**Q: Why do you need so much financial information?**
A: Buildings want to ensure residents can afford long-term housing costs. Detailed financials show your stability and reduce follow-up questions.

**Q: What if I don't have 2 years of rent in savings?**
A: That's a general guideline, not absolute requirement. Strong income, excellent credit, and good employment history can compensate. Explain your situation in cover letter.

**Q: Do I need to show all my bank accounts?**
A: Yes - show all accounts. The more assets you show, the stronger your application.

**Q: What if I have high debt?**
A: Be honest and disclose it. If your DTI is high, emphasize your strong income and plan to pay down debt. Consider paying down before applying.

**Q: Can I list family money as my assets?**
A: Only if it's in YOUR name (or joint with spouse). Parents' money is not your asset unless gifted to you (and documented).

---

### Documents

**Q: What format should documents be?**
A: PDF is best. JPG and PNG acceptable. Max 20 MB per file.

**Q: Can I use iPhone photos of documents?**
A: Yes, but ensure good quality: good lighting, flat surface, all corners visible, text legible. Scanner is better if available.

**Q: What if I don't have a document requested?**
A: Contact your broker. Some documents can be substituted (e.g., W-2 instead of pay stub).

**Q: How recent must bank statements be?**
A: Within 60-90 days. If older, request more recent statements from your bank.

---

### Employment

**Q: What if I just started a new job?**
A: That's fine. Include both new job and previous job to show employment history. If within first 3 months, include offer letter.

**Q: I'm self-employed - do I need to provide tax returns?**
A: Yes, last 2 years tax returns (full return with Schedule C). Also provide 1099s and business bank statements.

**Q: What if my employer doesn't allow employment verification calls?**
A: Request an employment verification letter from HR and upload it in Documents section.

**Q: I have a side gig - should I include it?**
A: Yes! More income strengthens your application. Add as separate employment record.

---

### Pets

**Q: Can I apply even if I don't have a pet yet?**
A: Yes, select "no pets" on application. If you want to get a pet later, you'll need board approval.

**Q: What if my pet doesn't meet building policy (breed, weight)?**
A: Contact building to request exception BEFORE applying. Some buildings are flexible, others are strict.

**Q: Do I need documentation for my service animal?**
A: Yes, please upload official certification or doctor's letter documenting need for service/emotional support animal.

**Q: What if I lie about having a pet?**
A: Don't! Undisclosed pets can result in lease violation, fines, or eviction. Always be honest.
